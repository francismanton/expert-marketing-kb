## Section 1 — Funnels & Funnel Selection
*Source: @brillaas*

### Framework: Funnel Type by Price Point

The fastest decision filter for funnel type is your offer price.

| Offer Price | Funnel Type |
|---|---|
| Under £1K | Low-ticket funnel |
| £1K–£3K | Webinar funnel |
| £3K–£10K | Call funnel OR webinar funnel |
| £10K+ | Call funnel OR challenge funnel |

**Rule:** Fix the offer first, then build the funnel around it. Without a compelling offer that cold traffic would want, you have no business running traffic.

---

### Framework: The Minimum Viable Call Funnel Stack

The moment you start overcomplicating a call funnel is the moment you lose. The complete required stack:

1. **Ads** — 15–30 unique, individual creatives
2. **VSL** — video sales letter on the landing page
3. **Landing page** — clean, direct, above-the-fold CTA
4. **Confirmation page** — trust assets + research materials
5. **Pre-call process** — setter outreach, framing, email flows
6. **Backend email flows** — 9–16 emails over 72 hours

**Build timeline:** 7–14 days maximum.

**New offer launch checklist:**
- Create V1 of program (first few modules — does NOT need to be complete)
- Set up Zoom for coaching calls + Slack for community
- Host course on Kajabi or Skool
- Build the funnel (14 days max)
- Record 15–30 ads
- Start booking calls

> *"The moment you start complicating your call funnel is the moment you lose."*

**How:** One specific avatar. Congruent funnel structure. Simple design. Explicitly articulated their pains and desires.

## Section 2 — Pixel Conditioning & Lead Quality
*Source: @brillaas + @lamxnt*

### Framework: The Core Lead Quality Problem

Unqualified leads are almost always a **pixel conditioning problem**, not an audience problem. The pixel is trained on the wrong people — and it compounds every day those ads run.

**Root cause:** Most funnels fire the pixel event on every opt-in or booking, including unqualified people. Meta then optimises toward finding more people like them.

**The Three-Part Fix:**

**1. Ad Messaging as a Filter**
- Write copy that repels bad leads before they click
- If your ad attracts everyone, it qualifies no one

**2. Landing Page Congruence**
- Messaging 100% aligned with the specific ad that brought them
- The page should further qualify, not just accept

**3. Application Conditional Routing**
- Use conditional logic so only qualified respondents reach the booking page
- Only qualified leads fire the pixel event (Lead, Schedule, Submit Application)
- This trains Meta to find more people like your qualified applicants

**Advanced:** CRM-triggered CAPI fires. Warm audience exclusions. Stack multiple standard events on the qualified page to access different audience pools.

> *"Your pixel is trained on the wrong people. And it gets worse with each day your ads are up."*

**Cross-reference:** Jeremy Haynes — DM Ads pixel conditioning via quiz funnel / schedule-event optimisation. Same principle, different funnel type.

---

### Insight: Market Language as a Lead Quality Signal
*Source: @lamxnt*

The language your market uses to describe their pain is better ad copy than anything a copywriter invents — because it bypasses the "this sounds like marketing" filter.

Most market research fails because the market doesn't yet have language to give accurate data at face value. You have to observe what they actually say, not ask them to describe it.

**Apply to messaging:** Use the exact phrases your best prospects use to describe their own problems — verbatim in ads, landing pages, and email subject lines.

## Section 3 — VSL & Landing Page Principles
*Source: @brillaas*

### Framework: VSL Philosophy for Paid Traffic (2026)

The "hook them in and reveal the offer later" format is **not working** right now, especially with affluent demographics. Frontload everything.

**Structure:**
- **First 60–180 seconds:** Give ALL information needed to decide — who this is for, why they should buy, what they get
- **Remainder:** Reiterate and expand for skeptical viewers

**Key principles:**
- Average VSL engagement rate is 20–30% — the first 60–180 seconds carry all conversion weight
- No reason to gate the button behind full consumption — put the offer in the first 1–3 minutes
- Affluent demographics want all information immediately; making them wait signals you're playing games

> *"The 'hook them in first' format isn't working right now — especially for affluent demographics."*

**Cross-reference:** Jeremy Haynes 2026 VSL section — consistent framework. See Haynes for button withhold decision and page structure detail.

---

### Framework: Landing Page Checklist for Paid Traffic

**Required elements (in priority order):**
- Clean branding and design — no clutter
- Direct messaging — no mystery or intrigue copy
- Clear offer — what they get, who it's for
- Specific pains/problems the ICP is experiencing right now
- FAQs: Who are you? What is this? What do I get? Why is this different?
- VSL that is a direct extension of the ad
- Mobile-optimised
- Headline, subhead, VSL, and CTA all above the fold

**Headline formula — four required elements:**
1. Named mechanism (your specific system/method)
2. Timeframe (30 days, 60 days, etc.)
3. The core outcome
4. Proof element

**Example:**
> *"In 30 Days Or Less, Our 'Open Market Wholesaling System' Will Help You Sign Your Next Contract & Close £10–20K Wholesale Deals — Proven Across 250+ Students"*

**Subheadline rule:** If your mechanism needs explaining, explain it in the subheadline. Confusion = no booking.

## Section 4 — Show Rates & Pre-Call Process
*Source: @brillaas*

### Framework: The Pre-Call Show Rate System

A properly executed pre-call process produces 65%+ show rates. Below 65%? One or more of these four components is missing.

**Component 1 — Setter Outreach (highest ROI per action)**
- Double-dial within 1–3 minutes of booking
- Send selfie video or voice note before calling
- Before dialling: text them to send "hey, it's [your name]"
- Call = providing resources + confirming burning questions
- Use iMessage / blue text (Sendblue or Linq) — signals you're human

**Component 2 — Confirmation Page**
- Frame it as a "research page" not just a scheduling confirmation
- Load with: trust assets, transparency videos, research materials
- Videos address: doubts, objections, common questions, what to expect
- This alone delivers ~17% average show rate improvement (Haynes benchmark)

**Component 3 — Backend Email Flows**
- 9–16 emails over 72 hours after booking
- Each email addresses a specific question or objection
- Redirect from emails back to confirmation page — drive consumption
- Goal: education + framing, not reminders

**Component 4 — Setter Nurture Between Booking and Call**
- Research the prospect, review their application data
- Send tailored resources based on what they shared
- Pre-call rapport makes post-call follow-up significantly easier

**Target:** 65%+ show rate. If below this with all four in place, check messaging congruence between ad and funnel.

> *"If your show rate is below 65% and you're not triple dialling leads, not sending voice notes, not sending selfie videos — you really don't want to solve the problem."*

**Cross-reference:** Jeremy Haynes "Hammer Them" campaign and four back-end selling systems for the paid retargeting layer.

## Section 5 — Offer Creation & Messaging
*Source: @lamxnt + @theroborourke*

### Framework: Marketing-First Offer Creation
*Source: @lamxnt*

The biggest offer creation mistake: build first, then market. Correct sequence: understand the ICP deeply first, then build the offer around what they actually want to buy.

**The principle:** If your offer genuinely solves a real market problem, your copy only needs to clearly lay out the transformation and articulate the mechanism. You shouldn't need to heavily convince anyone.

Heavy persuasion = symptom of a weak offer or a low-awareness market.

**Important nuance:** This is NOT "if you build it they will come." It means the excellence of the product came from understanding the market before building.

> *"The marketing actually comes first. Understanding the ICP, and THEN building the product."*

---

### Framework: The 10-Second Clarity Test
*Source: @theroborourke*

Your offer must be understood in 10 seconds. If your pitch requires qualifications or "let me break this down" — you've already lost them.

**The test:** Can you explain what pain you solve and how you solve it in one sentence?

| Bad | Good |
|---|---|
| "I provide UX consulting, design systems, user testing, KPI implementation, stakeholder management, and transformation strategy for enterprise SaaS companies" | "I help software companies that have lost track of what matters to get back on track with clear success metrics" |

When it's complicated, you're making the buyer do the work of figuring out why they should care. Buyers don't do that work.

---

### Framework: Specific-But-Universal Positioning
*Source: @theroborourke*

Great positioning pain points feel specific but apply universally. Everyone reads them and thinks "yes, that's me."

| Bad (too narrow) | Good (specific pain, universal application) |
|---|---|
| "Website redesigns for SaaS companies raising Series A" | "Websites that help you close deals you're currently losing to competitors" |

**Rule:** Position by pain and outcome, not by company stage or demographic. The right people self-select in.

---

### Framework: The Investment Multiplier Reframe
*Source: @theroborourke*

When selling strategy or foundation work, don't compete on monthly rates. Frame your investment as the multiplier that determines whether their ongoing costs produce a return.

**Example — designer selling £25K vs. £8K/month developer:**

> *"You're paying that developer £96K per year. For the next 12 months, they'll be building based on the strategy we create in the next 60 days. If that foundation is strong, you get maximum value from that £96K. If it's weak, you waste months rebuilding things that should have been right from the start. The £25K you invest now protects the £100K you're spending after."*

Your work is not competing with their ongoing costs — it's what determines whether those costs produce a return.

## Section 6 — AI-Integrated Marketing Systems
*Source: @lamxnt*

### Framework: Knowledge Management as AI Leverage

Knowledge management is one of the biggest levers for integrating AI into marketing operations. Most people use AI reactively. The better approach: build a systematic knowledge architecture that feeds AI agents continuously.

**The system:**
- Every decision, observation, and insight gets documented in a central knowledge base
- This becomes the training foundation for AI agents
- AI can then compare sales calls to ads, identify messaging patterns, analyse copy across campaigns — because everything is structured and searchable

**Build sequence (build one at a time, triggered by actual work):**
1. Build a research skill when you need research
2. Build a copy skill when you need copy
3. Wire them together so research feeds into copy
4. Expand incrementally — never speculatively

> *"Don't just download software for the sake of it. Build solutions to problems you're already experiencing."*

---

### Framework: The 8/10 Output Rule
*Source: @lamxnt*

A well-designed AI process consistently produces 8/10 output. The effort to push from 8/10 to 10/10 is rarely worth it — apply human effort to the final 20% of judgment and taste instead.

*"Hire the line cooks and garnish the plate yourself."*

The value is not in the AI tool — it's in the frameworks and training material you give the AI. The frameworks are the IP.

**Apply to:** Scaling ad creative volume, email sequences, VSL scripting, content production.

### Insight: Distribution as the Undervalued Business Moat
*Source: @lamxnt*

Most businesses fail not because of product quality, but because of failure to build predictable customer acquisition. Businesses that understand CAC:LTV and can generate demand at will lap their competition.

**The flywheel:** Proven acquisition → raise capital → reinvest into proven acquisition → accelerate.

**Apply when:** Client is focused on product improvement while acquisition is broken. The fix is almost never more product work.

## Section 7 — Positioning & Pricing
*Source: @theroborourke*

### Framework: The Phase Model (Escaping Hourly Billing)

Convert project components into phased deliverables priced on outcomes, not hours.

**The process:**
1. Review your last 2–3 projects
2. Identify what happens at the start of every project (setup, foundation, initial build) → Phase A
3. Identify Phase B, Phase C, Phase D in the same way
4. Keep a small hourly component only for genuine unknowns

**Example structure:**
> *"£8K for Phase A, £5K for Phase B, £9K for Phase C + D, plus 10 hours at £300/hr for adjustments."*

Same work. Same client. Moved from £10K hourly to £20K+ outcome-based.

---

### Framework: Pricing the Situation (Not the Work)

Don't price the work — price the situation.

**Three questions before pricing:**
1. What is their total budget?
2. What is the budget for this specific project?
3. Where does your work fit within that project budget?

Price what makes sense for the situation, then adjust scope to match.

---

### Insight: The 100% Close Rate Warning

A 100% close rate is a pricing problem, not a performance achievement.

**Healthy close rate: 65–75%.** Not 100%.

100% means prices are too low. You're only reaching people already sold before the call.

> *"If no one's saying no, you're leaving money on the table."*

---

### Insight: The Referral Pricing Trap

Referrals lock you into your current price bracket — when someone refers you, they tell the prospect what they paid. You're immediately anchored.

To break out: you need new buyers who don't know your old pricing, coming from content that establishes your new positioning first.

*"Referrals keep you alive. New positioning gets you to the next level."*

## Section 8 — Content Strategy & Authority
*Source: @theroborourke*

### Framework: The Proof + Value Formula

| Content type | Effect |
|---|---|
| Just value | Generic — can't distinguish you from anyone |
| Just proof | Looks like flexing |
| Proof + value | The proof gives weight to the lesson. The lesson justifies the proof. |

**Note:** The best selling content has lower engagement than viral content. You must choose: optimise for sales or optimise for being liked.

> *"Value without positioning is just noise. Positioning without value is just ego. You need both in every post."*

---

### Framework: Authority Signalling Without a Big Following

Premium buyers don't need you to have 100K followers. They need to see you operate at their level.

| Weak signal | Strong signal |
|---|---|
| "I just had a call with a client" | "I just reviewed a website for a startup doing 20,000 daily visitors" |

**For NDA-protected work:** Your *language* is the proof. Insider terminology signals you've been in the room — generic terms signal you haven't.

> *"When you can't show the work, get better at showing you understand the work."*

---

### Insight: The Silent Buyer Principle

For premium B2B deals: the bigger the client, the less they engage publicly. They watch in silence, then appear from nowhere with a booking.

~50% of premium B2B buyers have zero prior public engagement before reaching out.

Optimise for authority and trust-building, not engagement metrics.

---

### Insight: The Content Funnel Awareness Gap

Most service providers only create bottom-of-funnel content for the 2% already shopping. The other 98% have pain but haven't identified the problem yet.

**Top-of-funnel content structure:**
1. Talk about the pain your market feels
2. Show them what's causing it
3. Point toward the solution (without immediately pitching)

You educate them on why they need it before you sell it.

## Section 9 — Sales Call Methodology
*Source: @theroborourke*

### Framework: The 80/20 Sales Conversation Rule

Sales conversations: 80% understanding their business, 20% explaining your solution.

**Correct sequence:**
1. Stay on the business side — what are they trying to grow? Where are they losing deals? What's blocking their revenue goals?
2. Only once you've identified a live, painful business problem do you position your solution
3. No clear business pain = no sale

---

### Framework: The Two Extremes Technique (Breaking Stalls)

When a prospect is stalling, give two extreme options:

> *"Do you want to pause this completely and come back when timing is better — or do you want to try to get started in the next week or two?"*

People stall to avoid saying no directly. This provides an easy exit and reveals exactly where they stand.

---

### Framework: The Booking Form as the First Filter

Your booking form should disqualify people before they waste your time. Add: higher budget ranges, specific situation questions, business information fields.

When someone below your standard books → cancel before the call.

Goal: better calls, not more calls.

---

### Framework: The Testimonial Creation System

Asking clients to write their own testimonials produces generic results. ("Great to work with. 10/10." = zero sales power.)

**Better system:**
1. Ask: What was the situation before? What changed? Any metrics?
2. Write the before/after story yourself using their answers
3. Send back: "Does this sound right? Anything you want to change?"

This produces transformation-focused testimonials that convert — written by you, approved by them.
