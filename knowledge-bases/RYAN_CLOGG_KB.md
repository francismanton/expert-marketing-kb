## 1. CALL FUNNEL ARCHITECTURE

---
TITLE: The $70M VSSL Opt-In Call Funnel
SOURCES: E2, E25, E6, EP83, EP103, multiple videos
CATEGORY: Funnels
---

CORE CONCEPT:
Clogg's primary revenue engine for $70M+ in cash collected. A single VSSL opt-in funnel ran for years with minimal structural changes. The funnel's job is to pull the "unsold middle" (the vast majority who aren't yet convinced) toward "sold" through education, consumption, and social proof.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- Funnel flow: Meta Ad -> Opt-In Page -> VSSL Page -> Application (TypeForm/JotForm) -> Booking Page (Calendly) -> Confirmation Page (with breakout videos) -> Sales Call
- The same funnel supported offers from $497 info product to $7,200/yr coaching. Moving from $500-1,500 info to one-on-one coaching at the same price point through the SAME funnel jumped revenue from $1M/mo to $3M/mo -- without changing ads, pages, or traffic.
- Any funnel model can produce $12-15M/month if executed well. Don't chase trending formats; master one model.
- $77M collected across 5 funnels: main VSSL opt-in (primary), DTA variant, low-ticket ascension, challenge funnel, and webinar variant.
- Their funnel booked 119,473 calls over the business lifetime.
- 72% show rates achieved through confirmation page breakout videos + pre-call nurture sequences.
- VSSL play rate problem: when play rates are single/low-double digits, leads arrive to calls in "education mode" not "buying mode." Fix = back-end selling systems, not forcing VSSL consumption.

APPLY WHEN:
Default funnel architecture for any high-ticket info/coaching offer ($2K+). Start here before testing other models.

**Cross-reference:** Jeremy Haynes (VSSL structure, 2026 VSL). Daniel Fazio (VSSL as sales deck). brillaas (frontload philosophy). Sabri Suby (Secret Selling System).

## 2. LOW-TICKET ASCENSION FUNNEL

---
TITLE: Low-Ticket to High-Ticket Ascension Model
SOURCES: EP68 (Josh Gavin), E45, E22, E35, EP107
CATEGORY: Funnels / Offers
---

CORE CONCEPT:
A front-end low-ticket offer ($27-$97) self-liquidates ad spend and creates a warm buyer pool that ascends to high-ticket ($5K-$10K+) through setter calls. The low-ticket purchaser has demonstrated buying behavior, making them dramatically easier to close on the phone.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- Standard flow: Meta Ad -> 2-Step Order Form -> Checkout ($27-$49) -> Order Bump ($37-$97) -> OTO/Upsell ($97-$297) -> Thank You Page with "Book a Call" CTA -> Setter Call -> Closer Call
- 75% book-a-call take rate on thank-you page after low-ticket purchase (Maalouf data).
- Josh Gavin ($10M+ in low-ticket): "pocket ascension funnel" -- $34,161 in 6 days from test.
- Ascension rate from low-ticket to high-ticket: 40-78% depending on offer (Maalouf range).
- Challenge variant: $49 entry, $97 VIP upsell. Model: spend $200/lead -> $70 liquidated -> net $130 cost/lead -> each lead worth $250 average.
- Key insight (EP107): "Everyone copies the frontend... but the money is in the backend."
- Low-ticket retention risk: if too good as standalone, people never ascend. Must create desire for high-ticket, not satisfy the need.

APPLY WHEN:
When high-ticket CAC is too high for direct-to-call funnels, or when building a buyer list that ascends. Best for offers where front-end delivers quick wins creating appetite for full program.

**Cross-reference:** Russell Brunson (Value Ladder). Jeremy Haynes (low-to-high funnel type). Daniel Fazio (Type 1 vs Type 2 -- low-ticket = Type 1 economics).

## 3. DTA FUNNEL (DIRECT TO APPLICATION)

---
TITLE: DTA -- Direct To Application Funnel
SOURCES: EP105, expert interviews with Haynes, Maalouf
CATEGORY: Funnels
---

CORE CONCEPT:
Simplified variant: headline + VSSL + application button on same page (no opt-in step). Removes opt-in friction. Works best with warm organic audiences.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- Structure: Landing page with headline + embedded VSSL + application form/button on same page.
- Haynes advocates combining Calendly/application in one form to eliminate drop-off between apply and book.
- Best for warm organic traffic. One operator: 90%+ show rate and 50% close rate on mid-ticket with extremely warm audience.
- DTA vs. Opt-In debate: Haynes says opt-in funnels = "you hate money." Nick Fisher disagrees -- you need consumption to warm sophisticated buyers. Both work; depends on traffic temperature.
- Clogg runs both: VSSL opt-in (cold traffic primary), DTA (warm/retargeting secondary).

APPLY WHEN:
When traffic is warm (organic audience, retargeting, email list). Not recommended as primary for cold paid traffic.

## 4. BACK-END SELLING SYSTEMS (SHOW RATE ENGINEERING)

---
TITLE: Jeremy Haynes' 4 Back-End Selling Systems (validated on Clogg channel)
SOURCES: EP105 (Haynes interview), E36, E25
CATEGORY: Conversion / Show Rates
---

CORE CONCEPT:
The gap between booking and closing is the biggest lever in any call funnel. Haynes' 4 systems, each independently creating massive lift, took one client from 36% to 74% show rate in 1.5 weeks.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- **System 1: Confirmation Page Breakout Videos** -- Short FAQ videos answering purchase objections. +32% and +36% show rate lifts from this alone in two separate cases.
- **System 2: Value-Dense Email Sequences** -- Dense information (not sales pitches) that moves people along the sales process before the call.
- **System 3: "Hammer Them" Campaign** -- Excessive content (long + short form) blasted at leads who take key actions.
- **System 4: Manual Outreach by Sales People** -- Setters personally reaching out before the call.
- Combined: 36% to 74% show rate in 1.5 weeks, generating $178,000.
- Interest Spectrum: Curious -> General Interest -> Highly Interested -> Convicted. Above Convicted is where deals close. Information consumption dynamically moves leads up/down.
- "People show up to a sales call with a lack of information. It's no longer a sales call, it's an education call."

APPLY WHEN:
Immediately. If show rate is below 60% on paid traffic, implement System 1 (confirmation page videos) first -- fastest to deploy, biggest standalone impact.

**Cross-reference:** Jeremy Haynes (this IS Haynes' framework). brillaas (pre-call checklist). Ravi Abuvala (show-up rate system).

## 5. PIXEL STRATEGY & FINANCIAL QUALIFIER ROUTING

---
TITLE: $15M Meta Pixel Architecture
SOURCES: E19, E24 ($31K loss post-mortem)
CATEGORY: Paid Ads / Pixel
---

CORE CONCEPT:
Who you send to your pixel is who your algorithm finds more of. Separate pixels per funnel type, financial qualifiers to route DQ leads away from pixel training, browser + server event matching for de-duplication.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- **Two separate pixels**: VSSL/call funnel pixel vs. low-ticket funnel pixel. Never cross-pollinate.
- **Browser + server events**: Match for each key event. Lines aligned = de-duplicated, high-quality signal.
- **VSSL events**: Lead (opt-in) -> Submit Application (app completion) -> Schedule (booking)
- **Low-ticket events**: Lead (page 2 checkout) -> Initiate Checkout (JS on payment form) -> Purchase (success page)
- **Financial qualifier routing**: App question on investment history. <$1K = DQ path -- NO pixel event fires. All other buckets fire normally. <$1K closes at sub-5%; qualified buckets 10-12%.
- **Optimize for Purchase, not Schedule** as primary FB optimization target.
- **High-ticket purchase tracking workaround**: Sales close -> rep sends terms link with hidden JotForm -> fires browser + server event -> passes as Purchase to correct pixel.
- **Separate pixels per funnel angle**: different angles get own pixel + ad account.
- Use DEFAULT conversion events (not custom) -- pools with all FB advertisers, improving algo.
- **$31K loss post-mortem bugs**: Schedule-optimization pollution, DQ leads in automation, multi-select qualifier bug, broken Calendly-Zoom links, gender targeting error, split test routing error.

APPLY WHEN:
When running paid Meta/Google at $5K+/day. Financial qualifier routing alone can transform lead quality.

**Cross-reference:** Jeremy Haynes (pixel conditioning). brillaas (pixel conditioning). Eddie Maalouf (credit-check integration -- advanced).

## 6. SALES OPERATIONS & SETTER ARCHITECTURE

---
TITLE: Tiered Setter System + Sales Ops
SOURCES: E48, E21, E44, EP106, EP66, EP84
CATEGORY: Sales
---

CORE CONCEPT:
Adding setters was Clogg's most impactful operational unlock -- after $70M with zero setters. System categorizes leads by source with distinct benchmarks.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- **Auto Set** (post-purchase -> direct setter booking): 76-78% show, 20%+ close
- **Self Set** (setter dials CRM app-no-books): 61% show, 12% close
- **Confirm Set** (confirmation calls on pipeline): 69% show, 14% close
- **App no-book rate: ~50%.** At scale = 200-300/day. Largest recoverable revenue source.
- **Mandy AI**: Texts app-no-books within 5 min. Runs 24/7. Flags human-needed conversations to Slack.
- **Bell system**: <$1K = no bell -> $20K+ = 4 bells. Setters prioritize highest first.
- **"Mark as free" technique**: Non-confirmed slots freed via API for double-booking.
- **SPB**: 15% great, 13% avg, 10% min. Below 10% = problem.
- First 5 days with setters: $34,700 from 6 units.
- **Josh Troy (WFS) outsourced model**: 20% flat, all-inclusive. Mgmt ratio 1:2-3 (competitors 1:20).
- **In-house costs 18-25%+ fully loaded.** Agency at 20% covers everything.
- **Never make your top rep your manager.** All operators agree independently.

APPLY WHEN:
Any call funnel doing 50+ bookings/day. Start with Auto Set, add Self Set, then Confirm Set.

**Cross-reference:** Daniel Fazio (two-sale model). theroborourke (sales methodology). Jeremy Haynes (group closing).

## 7. SALES TEAM COMPENSATION

---
TITLE: W2 Tiered Commission System ($30M paid to reps)
SOURCES: E13, E26, E5, EP106
CATEGORY: Sales / Compensation
---

CORE CONCEPT:
Compensation architecture for 30+ person sales team processing $70M+. Tiered by rolling SPB with point-based bonuses.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- **Base**: $1,692/two weeks (~$44K annualized). Draw: commission replaces base when it exceeds.
- **Tiered commission (60-day rolling SPB)**: Junior 8%, Adviser 9%, Senior 10% (on net cash, first 30 days).
- **Point bonuses per 2-week period** ($1 weekday = 1pt, $1 weekend = 1.5pt):
  - 60K pts ($60K+) -> $3K bonus | 100K pts -> $6K | 140K pts -> $9K | 180K pts -> $12K
- **Top rep**: $300-400K+/year.
- **Clawback**: First 30 days only. After 30 = company absorbs.
- **PIFF 30-day incentive**: Commission on payment-plan-to-PIF conversions within 30 days.
- **Stripe automation**: Unique order form per rep -> Zap tags Stripe customer with Rep ID -> all charges/refunds auto-attributed. Works to $4M/mo.
- **Comp benchmarks**: Setters $5-8K OTE, Closers $10-15K min ($30K+ A-players), Managers $15-30K.

APPLY WHEN:
When building/restructuring sales team. Build Stripe automation on Day 1 of hiring rep #1.

## 9. LTV/CAC MATH & UNIT ECONOMICS

---
TITLE: LTV to CAC Ratio Deep Dive
SOURCES: E47, E6, EP97, E30
CATEGORY: Financial
---

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- **CAC (B2C info/coaching Meta)**: $200-400/booked call x 50-60% show = $333-666/live call. At 20% close, CAC = $1,665-$3,330.
- **LTV**: $7,200 PIF Y1 + 40-60% renewal Y2 + Y3 retention = $10,800-$15,000+ LTV. At $3K CAC = 3.6-5x ratio.
- **Profit reality**: $70M revenue, 20-30% margin at scale = ~$14-21M profit lifetime.
- **10x LTV:CAC**: 3-5 levers to increase LTV (renewals, upsells, ascension, payment recovery, referrals) + 3-5 to decrease CAC (creative, show rates, close rates, pixel, setter recovery).

## 13. CONTRADICTIONS & TENSIONS

### DTA vs. Opt-In Funnel (Haynes vs. Fisher)
- **Resolution:** DTA for warm traffic. Opt-in + VSSL consumption for cold paid. Run both.

### In-House vs. Outsourced Sales
- **Resolution:** Outsource at 20% when $200K+/mo and cash-flow business. Build in-house only for exit.

### W2 vs. 1099 Reps (Clogg's own tension)
- **Clogg's verdict:** "I still don't know if W2 was a good call."
- **Resolution:** 1099 for cash-flow. W2 only if building for exit.

### AI-Lean vs. Large Team (Fazio vs. Clogg)
- **Resolution:** Different models. Fazio = B2B text services (AI-automatable). Clogg = B2C coaching (human coaches, sales teams). Right model depends on fulfillment type.
