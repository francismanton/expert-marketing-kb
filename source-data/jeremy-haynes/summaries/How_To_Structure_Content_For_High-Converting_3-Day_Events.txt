TITLE: How To Structure Content For High-Converting 3-Day Events
VIDEO: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gC5aKqhR0Cg
CATEGORY: Funnels - Challenge
============================================================

CORE CONCEPT:
The optimal structure for challenge funnels and 3-day virtual events follows a specific content flow: Day 1 is all content (get them in the game + further awareness), Day 2 is half content plus pre-pitch framing and the main pitch, and Day 3 opens with a rep pitch, silo pitch for higher-level buyers, and closes with a content wrap-up.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- Day 1 opens with "get them in the game" content — origination story, why the topic matters, and foundational education. Then progress into deeper teaching sessions that give attendees enough understanding to feel they got value from their ticket purchase. Day 1 typically runs 3-6 hours.
- Day 2 first half brings in supporting speakers who present the same central topic from completely different perspectives (different industries, different applications, different outcomes). This makes the offer feel well-rounded and handles objections indirectly.
- Day 2 second half uses Myron Golden's pre-pitch framework — a series of 1-3 minute stories reframing success characteristics, risk-taking, and time compression that prime attendees to buy before the actual pitch. Then deliver the main offer pitch and end the day.
- Day 3 opens with a direct rep pitch calling out why people didn't buy, demonizing the "small self," and using hand-raise mechanics to get people to publicly commit. Have a second speaker do their own rep pitch version as well.
- Between Day 2 and Day 3, offer a "silo pitch" — a separate session for higher-level attendees (e.g., those already at $1M+) who self-identify for a premium offer at a higher price point, creating an additional revenue stream beyond the main offer.

KEY QUOTES:
- "Outcomes don't always have to be an actual tangible result. Sometimes all that has to happen is making them feel like they understand it thoroughly enough to make a decision."
- "All it takes is one little aha moment — that gives customers the satisfaction that they got what they're there for."

APPLY WHEN:
You're designing the actual content schedule and speaker lineup for a challenge funnel or 3-day virtual event. Use this to map out what to cover each day, when to bring in supporting speakers, and how to structure the pre-pitch and pitch sequence for maximum conversions.
