TITLE: How To Create & Sell High-Ticket Recurring Offers (Full Breakdown)
VIDEO: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eZ2u4giDkvY
CATEGORY: Offers
============================================================

CORE CONCEPT:
High-ticket recurring revenue ($5K-$10K+/month) is achievable when you can produce measurable results for clients within every 30-day billing cycle. The key is starting at a fair price, incrementally raising it as your certainty and results improve, and building an offer stack with fresh/new material that justifies ongoing payment — transforming your business from starting at zero each month to waking up with substantial baseline revenue.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- The #1 requirement for recurring high-ticket is producing ongoing results that exceed the charge within each 30-day period. Track the specific metric that represents the value clients extract (monetary return, speed to result, access value) and optimize around accelerating that outcome.
- Start your recurring price lower than where you want to end up — this is about matching price to your current certainty level. Jeremy started at $1,500/month and incrementally raised to $2K, $3K, $4K, $5K, $6K, then $10K/month as results compounded, systems improved, and member quality increased. Pricing reflects certainty.
- The offer stack should include multiple consumption formats that are updated regularly: AI trained on all your material (updated daily), live calls (weekly/biweekly), SOPs (monthly), community/group chat (daily engagement), course vault (weekly updates), and in-person events (quarterly). Each element should be fresh/new on a recurring basis.
- Higher pricing attracts better clients who consume faster, engage more, get results faster, stay longer, and refer higher-quality people. At $10K/month vs. $1,500/month, activation rate is dramatically higher — 100% login rate at $5K vs. low engagement at $200. Add friction (annual commitment, income qualifications) to filter for the right people.
- Frame the milestone math: 200 people at $5K/month = $1M/month recurring. Even starting small with tens of thousands in recurring revenue can liquidate your entire ad spend, letting all new transactional sales become pure profit. Annualized churn of 3.4-3.7% is the benchmark to aim for.

KEY QUOTES:
- "You want to charge somebody every 30 days, you have to exceed the value of what they paid — whether it be a monetary return, status, clarity, whatever it is."
- "Pricing is reflected with the certainty of your system for producing those ongoing results."
- "It's likely going to be a difference in audience that you're selling to now — the people who were very first a part of the group are not the people that are a part of it today."

APPLY WHEN:
Transitioning from transactional high-ticket sales to recurring revenue, designing a mastermind/community offer, or trying to reduce revenue volatility by building a recurring base. Essential for anyone currently doing one-time $3K-$10K+ sales who wants predictable monthly revenue and better client outcomes through ongoing engagement.
