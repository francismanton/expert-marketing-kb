CORE CONCEPT:
The difference between businesses that scale to $1M/month and those that stall comes down to a handful of behavioral patterns: knowing when to adjust your risk profile upward, avoiding "split testing your way to poverty," practicing the art of detachment from what's no longer working, and solving problems at maximum speed rather than tolerating bottlenecks.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- Adjust your risk profile upward by reinvesting profits ("house money") into new funnels or higher ad spend rather than pocketing early gains. Once you pull your initial risk capital back, aggressively gamble the profit on expansion — new funnel types, more closers, maxed calendars.
- Stop split testing your way to poverty. If you're at a 14:1 ROAS down from 17:1, do the math — spending more at a lower ROAS often nets far more profit than pausing to optimize back to the higher ratio. Comparison bias (e.g., wanting 80% margins when you're netting 40%) kills scale potential.
- Manage credit lines proactively. Have multiple backup payment methods (Chase, Citi, not just Amex). A client lost hundreds of thousands in revenue because their $995K Facebook invoicing balance hit the limit on a Saturday and wires don't process until Monday. Five days of zero ad spend destroyed sales team morale and pipeline.
- Practice the art of detachment. When a funnel, creative, team member, or strategy stops producing, detach and rotate — don't keep feeding Zuckerberg's pocket with emotional attachment to what used to work. This applies to ad fatigue, funnel decay, and even personnel.
- Solve problems at maximum speed — "quest stack" them. All problems are inevitable regardless of pace; choosing to move slowly just spreads the same pain over a longer timeline. The speed at which you solve problems directly dictates the speed of success.

KEY QUOTES:
- "I do not want to be right. I just want to make money."
- "Don't split test your way to poverty."
- "The problems are all there. They're inevitable. Why not quest stack and blitz through them?"

APPLY WHEN:
Reference this when a client is profitable but hesitant to scale — sitting on good ROAS but afraid to spend more, clinging to a funnel or creative that's declining, or moving too slowly through operational problems. Also applicable when a client needs a mindset shift from optimizing margins to maximizing net dollars.
