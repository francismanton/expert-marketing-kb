TITLE: Non-Obvious Ways To Dramatically Increase Your Sales Team's Performance
VIDEO: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lFvxOQCU0Dk
CATEGORY: Sales
============================================================

CORE CONCEPT:
Sales team underperformance often stems from non-obvious factors like authority transfer failure, lack of specialized knowledge transfer, toxic narrative management, and blame culture — not just closing skills. Fixing these foundational issues creates double-digit improvements in close rates.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- Ensure authority transfers from you to your sales team by making your reps look the part — branded polos, professional Zoom environments, visible success lifestyle, and searchable online presence aligned to your organization. A high-authority founder paired with low-authority reps kills deals.
- Rep names and phone numbers matter more than you think. One client saw a 30% show rate difference between a rep named "David" (70% show rate) and a rep with a complex multi-syllable name (40%). Use iPhones for texting (not blue-text software companies), match communication platform to demographic (WhatsApp for international, iMessage for Americans).
- Transfer specialized knowledge into your sales team through consistent training, culture immersion, team meetings, and filming training content. When questions arise that reps can't answer, have a "phone an expert" system where you or a senior staff member can jump on the call in real-time.
- Attack narrative management aggressively. When sales teams develop a storyline that "leads suck," it becomes self-fulfilling. One client's team stopped closing after switching to a shorter application — the fix was literally lying to the team that the old long application was restored. Close rates immediately recovered, proving the narrative was the problem.
- Replace blame culture with extreme ownership. When sales teams blame marketing, they surrender the ability to influence outcomes. The best-performing organizations have sales teams that point the finger at themselves, unlocking creative solutions and performance improvements.

KEY QUOTES:
- "When your people look like shit, that authority from you into them is going to fail to transfer."
- "The narrative has to be attacked aggressively. You cannot have a narrative just go on and run wild because as it spreads, it cements itself more."
- "As soon as somebody starts playing the blame game, it's cooked. There is no accountability."

APPLY WHEN:
Your sales team is underperforming despite having full calendars and decent lead quality. Use this diagnostic framework when close rates have dropped and the sales team is blaming marketing — the real issues are likely authority transfer, narrative management, or training gaps rather than lead quality problems.
