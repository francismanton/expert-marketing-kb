TITLE: How To Maximize Show-Up Rates For Challenge Funnels
VIDEO: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6eus1rfs0gs
CATEGORY: Funnels - Challenge
============================================================

CORE CONCEPT:
Maximizing show-up rates for challenge funnels requires understanding that buyers are still unsold after purchasing — they're in "justification mode," not "scanner mode." The solution is deploying all four back-end selling systems (Hammer Them, value-dense emails, confirmation page breakout videos, and setter outreach) not just before Day 1, but between each day of the event to maintain attendance through Day 3.

TACTICAL TAKEAWAYS:
- Deploy the Hammer Them strategy immediately after ticket purchase: upload buyer lists and retarget with 30-50 short-form and 15-30 long-form content pieces organized around four quadrants — questions, second-layer questions, objections, and expectations. Run as engagement campaigns optimized for ThruPlay video views. This keeps you in front of buyers from purchase through event day, preventing them from forgetting.
- Send value-dense email sequences, not just reminder emails. Put the actual valuable content IN the email body — don't rely on clicks. Email open rates are 50-70%, but click-through rates are single digits. Write emails that educate, raise interest levels, and get people excited to attend. Calendar invites and reminders are necessary but insufficient alone.
- Use confirmation page breakout videos (1-3 minutes each, 3-19 per page) to nurture trust immediately after purchase. Prospects have just shifted from scanner mode to justification mode — they're actively seeking information to validate their decision. Cover the most common things people need to understand right after opting in.
- Setter/SDR outreach should be personalized: research the buyer online, review application data, ask strategic questions about their interest, then send a tailored list of resources and sales assets. Establish pre-event communication so that after the event, the setter already has rapport for follow-up. Offer to be their support contact.
- Repeat ALL back-end selling systems between each day: Day 1→Day 2 and Day 2→Day 3. Blast Hammer Them content, have setters reach out asking about the previous day, send value-dense email updates, and update the dashboard with new confirmation page assets. Day 2 pitch should emphasize "Day 2 is the most important day" to drive attendance.

KEY QUOTES:
- "On the front end, people are in scanner mode. On the back end, they're in the justification phase — that's where high-intent consumption actually occurs."
- "If you remain in the mindset that they're already sold because they already bought a ticket, that's why you don't have 100% show rates."
- "The right information leads to action. The wrong information or the lack of information leads to inaction."

APPLY WHEN:
Running a challenge funnel or 3-day virtual event and show-up rates are below 40-50%, or when you're seeing significant drop-off between Day 1 and Day 3. Also essential during the planning phase of any multi-day event to build the full back-end selling system infrastructure before promotion begins.
